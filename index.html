About Me
Programming is a deep and fascinating field. I find myself lucky to be in a place where I get to code day in and day out. The logic, creativity, patience and dedication it takes to bring a project together can seem like a large mountain to climb sometimes. But the reward of seeing a piece of code do its job exactly as you programmed it to is one of the most satisfying feelings there is.

I was introduced to Programming in the 10th grade. We were all given TI-83's and asked to write a program that solved a simple equation. I was hooked there and then. I spent the next few evenings filling my calculator with all the equations I needed for the next test, just because it was so interesting to create them.

From that time on, programming would be a hobby to me. I went through college as a philosophy student (I was planning on becoming a priest... until my wife came along). My classes though never really held my attention though. I always found myself working on some game in the back of the class.

After graduation, I went to work for a called US Airweld, where I did Hydrostatic Pressure Testing of high-pressure cylinders. The best thing this job had to offer was a lot of free time to think, which I often used to contemplate the different programs I was working on.

That company eventually traded hands and I eventually ended up getting a referral from a friend to come be a programmer for the Paradise Valley School District. A place I have happily spent the last 4 years working on a student database of about 34,000 students.This job has provided a lot of interesting projects for me and I have grown a lot through the great opportunities they have given me.

Looking to the future, I am very excited by Big Data and the emerging field of Augmented Reality. The rich possibilities of both of these fields look to be able to produce a lot of good for the world and I am hoping to find a way to help where I can.
